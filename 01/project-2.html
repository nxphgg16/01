Designing a social network application for the Golf player community

Project overview:

The golf app in question is a comprehensive tool for golf enthusiasts, providing features such as score tracking, course information, and social interaction for golfers of all skill levels

My roles: Visual Design, Prototype

Duration: 1 Apr 2023 - 15 May 2023

Tool: Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, Gmail

Challenge:

Taking over the later stages of the design process. Our mission was to transition from low-fi wireframes to hi-fi mockups with visually impressive and user-centered designs. To be successful, we need to solve several design challenges:

Why is this a problem?

The research goals are focused on understanding customer needs, pain points, and preferences to inform the design and development process.

Users have difficulty connecting with the community

Users desired more opportunities for social interaction within the app, such as connecting with friends, arranging games, or sharing scores.

Non-intuitive point tracking

Unintuitive score tracking led to frequent data entry errors and inefficiencies.

Visual design app

Outdated visual design reduces user engagement because it fails to create a modern and engaging visual experience.

solution:

1. Social Interaction Features

Social features, such as friend connections, game organization, and score sharing, were integrated into the app to facilitate community building and engagement.

2. Enhanced Course Information

Comprehensive course information is now presented in an organized manner, including hole layouts, distances, user reviews, and visual aids such as maps and photos.

3. Intuitive Score Tracking

The scorecard feature was redesigned with user-friendly input fields, auto-suggestions, and a more straightforward data entry process.

4. Online platform for buying and selling golf-related products

Places users can evaluate, compare prices, product quality and find affordable prices. Providing a safe and secure trading market that can be trusted with the process.

usability testing:

To test usability, we conducted an unmoderated testing method. With 5 users who regularly play golf. This allows usability issues to be addressed early.

Task 1: Ask the user to post an article and a scorecard

Task 2: Ask the user to find a golf course

Task 3: Ask the user to create an even

Task 4: Ask the user to post an item for sale and contact to buy an item

Task 5: Ask users to text or call others

Task 6: Ask users to check personal information changes

insight:

Insight 1:When users want to post something, they usually look toward the top of the home page

Insight 2:Many users believe that the Create List button is not too important when arranging with the current local

Insight 3:All users said that the position of the elements on the header was unfamiliar when used

Takeaways:

Communication:If I can do the project again, I will definitely jump out of my comfort zone and get to know what others are doing, and of course, ask questions

Design:Design system matters! We started working on sketch without a design system, and didn’t expect there are so many screens, and very quickly we notice some inconsistency, since when we made changes on a screen, we sometimes forgot to change other screens accordingly. After learning the concept of atomic design, we realized the importance of defining a design system before hand

Prioritize:Knowing what not to do is just as important as knowing what to do within a limited amount of time. Our minimum viable product may not be feature-perfect, but if I can continue, I will definitely learn from experience and plan the upgrade steps

Final Thoughts:

Working with a start-up in the US was truly a great experience for me. Being part of the overall design process is really exciting and collaborating with people from different cultures. A clear way of working and process is highly appreciated. Throughout this process, we discovered valuable insights and considerations that had a profound impact on the success of the application.
